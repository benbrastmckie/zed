**PATIENT 1 --- RESOLUTION (Post-Poll 2)**

**Labs returned:** HIV VL undetectable despite being 10 weeks late for CAB and 4 weeks late for LEN.

**Two teaching points (present in this order):**

**Point 1 --- The low-barrier philosophy:** In low-barrier settings, we act with the opportunity in front of us. Don\'t wait for perfect lab data. That delay = further delay = months of lost contact in this population.

**Point 2 --- Why LEN likely protected him:** LEN has a longer pharmacokinetic tail than CAB. When CAB went subtherapeutic, LEN (capsid inhibitor, different mechanism) likely continued to suppress. Without LEN, INSTI resistance was almost certain during a 10-week CAB gap. LEN may have prevented that.

**Restart:** LEN + CAB monthly restarted.

**Phase 2: Success (again)**

- 16 more injections over \~16 months

- All on time (MXM or outreach RN)

- VL undetectable throughout

**Published:** Mehtani NJ et al. \"HIV Viral Suppression With Unintentional Lenacapavir Monotherapy in Adherence-Challenged Patients: A Case Series.\" Open Forum Infect Dis. 2025;12(6):ofaf330. PMC12188974. (Patient 1 = Patient B in paper)

**PATIENT 1 --- FINAL UPDATE (2nd LTFU)**

**Current status:**

- Disappeared again after 16 more months of success

- 11 weeks since last CAB/RPV dose

- Still within LEN window (due at \~26 weeks)

- Cannot locate --- suspected \>3,000 miles away based on Care Everywhere ED note

- Local shelter outreach unsuccessful

- **Officially LTFU --- the only patient in our cohort to reach this point**

**Silver lining (hypothesis):** If resistance develops during this lapse, it will likely be to LEN --- not to the INSTI class. LEN\'s long tail means CAB may still be offering some INSTI protection. If/when he re-engages anywhere, INSTI-based options may still be available.

**Population-level question to raise:** As LA-ART expands beyond HIV specialty centers into community-based settings, should LEN + CAB/RPV become the DEFAULT for patients at high LTFU risk --- as a harm reduction strategy to protect the INSTI class at the population level?

**Supporting data point:** At MXM/WPIC/shelters, we have encountered at least 2 OTHER patients started on CAB/RPV alone at other counties, who became LTFU, presented to MXM months later with new INSTI resistance. That\'s devastating. LEN might have prevented it.

**Downsides of LEN:** Painful SC injection, harder to administer than IM CAB/RPV.

**SECTION 4: KEY TAKEAWAYS (closing slide)**

1.  LA-ART works in this population --- 100% suppression --- but requires intensive infrastructure. It is not a set-it-and-forget-it intervention.

2.  For patients at high LTFU risk, LEN + CAB/RPV may offer population-level harm reduction --- protecting the INSTI class even when CAB levels drop.

3.  In low-barrier settings: act when the patient is in front of you. Waiting for perfect lab data can mean waiting forever.

4.  Starting ART in patients with AIDS and incompletely treated infectious pericarditis raises novel IRIS concerns that the field has not yet addressed.

5.  Humility. We are working in genuinely new territory. These cases don\'t have clean endings --- and that\'s the point.
