<div class="teaching-point">
<strong>Teaching point 1 -- Low-barrier philosophy:</strong> In low-barrier settings, act with the opportunity in front of you. Don't wait for perfect lab data. That delay = further delay = months of lost contact.
</div>

<div class="teaching-point">
<strong>Teaching point 2 -- Why LEN likely protected him:</strong> LEN has a longer pharmacokinetic tail than CAB. When CAB went subtherapeutic, LEN (capsid inhibitor, different mechanism) likely continued to suppress. Without LEN, INSTI resistance was almost certain during a 10-week CAB gap.
</div>

</v-click>

<div style="padding: 1.5rem 2.5rem;">

# Key Takeaways

<div class="takeaways">

<ol>

<li>LA-ART works in this population -- <strong>100% suppression</strong> -- but requires intensive infrastructure. It is not a set-it-and-forget-it intervention.</li>

<li>For patients at high LTFU risk, <strong>LEN + CAB/RPV may offer population-level harm reduction</strong> -- protecting the INSTI class even when CAB levels drop.</li>

<li>In low-barrier settings: <strong>act when the patient is in front of you.</strong> Waiting for perfect lab data can mean waiting forever.</li>

<li>Starting ART in patients with AIDS and incompletely treated infectious pericarditis raises <strong>novel IRIS concerns</strong> that the field has not yet addressed.</li>

<li><strong>Humility.</strong> We are working in genuinely new territory. These cases don't have clean endings -- and that's the point.</li>

</ol>
