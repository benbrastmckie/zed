## Executive Summary

This is a clinical grand rounds presentation on long-acting antiretroviral therapy (LA-ART) and long-acting PrEP (LA-PrEP) delivery through the MXM street medicine program at UCSF/ZSFG. The talk opens with program-level outcome data (n=34 LA-ART, n=52 LA-PrEP), then proceeds through four patient cases that illustrate real-world clinical dilemmas in managing LA-ART for people experiencing homelessness. Four interactive Poll Everywhere questions engage the audience in clinical decision-making. The overarching message: LA-ART works in this population (100% viral suppression), but requires intensive infrastructure, flexible clinical reasoning, and population-level thinking about resistance protection via lenacapavir.

### Slide 11: Patient 1 -- Resolution + LEN Pharmacology

**Template**: Layout 28 (Bullet -- White)
**Status**: mapped

**Content**:

**What we did**: D -- Administered LEN + CAB same day

**Labs returned**: HIV VL **undetectable** despite 10 weeks late for CAB, 4 weeks late for LEN

**Teaching Point 1 -- Low-barrier philosophy**:
In low-barrier settings, act with the opportunity in front of you. Don't wait for perfect lab data. Delay = further delay = months of lost contact.

**Teaching Point 2 -- Why LEN likely protected him**:
LEN has a longer pharmacokinetic tail than CAB. When CAB went subtherapeutic, LEN (capsid inhibitor, different MOA) likely continued to suppress. Without LEN, INSTI resistance was almost certain during a 10-week CAB gap.

**Phase 2**: Restarted LEN + CAB monthly. 16 more injections over ~16 months, all on time, VL undetectable throughout.

Published: Mehtani et al., Open Forum Infect Dis 2025 (PMC12188974)

**Speaker Notes**:
So we gave him both injections that day. And when the labs came back -- undetectable. Despite being 10 weeks late for CAB. Two things to take away here. First: in this population, the window of engagement IS the moment of opportunity. If we'd sent him away for labs, he might have disappeared for months. Second: this is the LEN story. LEN has a much longer pharmacokinetic tail than CAB. When CAB dropped below therapeutic levels, LEN -- which works by a completely different mechanism, capsid inhibition -- was likely still providing coverage. Without LEN, we'd almost certainly be looking at INSTI resistance after a 10-week gap. Instead, he restarted and did another 16 months perfectly.
