## Executive Summary

This is a 20-25 minute conference presentation on long-acting antiretroviral therapy (LA-ART) and long-acting PrEP (LA-PrEP) delivery through the MXM clinic serving people experiencing homelessness and substance use disorders in San Francisco. The talk opens with program-level outcome data (100% viral suppression in n=34, oral vs. LA-ART comparison), then presents four clinical cases with audience polling to illustrate real-world complexities: NNRTI resistance driving regimen selection, lenacapavir as INSTI-class protection during lapses, cross-county care coordination for unstable patients, and IRIS risk with severe immunosuppression. The overarching message is that low-barrier LA-ART works but demands infrastructure, flexibility, and clinical humility.
