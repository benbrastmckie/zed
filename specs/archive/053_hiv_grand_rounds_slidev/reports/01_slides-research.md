## Executive Summary

This is a case-based HIV Grand Rounds presentation covering MXM's long-acting ART and PrEP programs. The talk opens with two data overview sections (LA-ART cohort n=34, LA-PrEP cohort n=68 prescribed/52 initiated), then presents four patient cases with interactive Poll Everywhere questions. The core clinical arguments are: (1) 100% viral suppression in a deeply marginalized cohort, (2) lenacapavir as population-level INSTI class protection for LTFU-risk patients, and (3) low-barrier philosophy -- act when the patient is in front of you. The talk closes with a genuine invitation for clinical dialogue on novel IRIS questions.

### Slide 11: Patient 1 -- Resolution + LEN Pharmacology

**Template**: custom (bullet with stat callout)
**Status**: mapped

**Content**:

Labs returned: **VL undetectable** despite 10 weeks late for CAB, 4 weeks late for LEN

**Teaching point 1 -- Low-barrier philosophy:**
In low-barrier settings, act with the opportunity in front of you. Don't wait for perfect lab data. That delay = further delay = months of lost contact.

**Teaching point 2 -- Why LEN likely protected him:**
LEN has a longer pharmacokinetic tail than CAB. When CAB went subtherapeutic, LEN (capsid inhibitor, different mechanism) likely continued to suppress. Without LEN, INSTI resistance was almost certain during a 10-week CAB gap.

Phase 2 -- Success (again): 16 more injections over ~16 months, all on time, VL undetectable throughout.

Published: Mehtani NJ et al., Open Forum Infect Dis 2025;12(6):ofaf330. PMC12188974.

**Speaker Notes**:
So we gave him LEN plus CAB that day. Labs came back -- VL undetectable. Despite being ten weeks late. And I think LEN is why. LEN has a longer pharmacokinetic tail than cabotegravir. When CAB went subtherapeutic, the capsid inhibitor was still working. Without it, I think INSTI resistance during a ten-week gap was almost certain. He then did another sixteen months on time, VL undetectable throughout.
